Sharing PDF documents has never been easier than with a **QR code for PDF**. Instead of emailing large attachments, printing stacks of paper, or hoping someone types a URL correctly, you can simply let people scan a code and instantly view your document on their phone or tablet.

From restaurant menus and product catalogs to instruction manuals and event programs, PDF QR codes are transforming how businesses share information. This guide covers everything you need to know about creating, customizing, and deploying QR codes that link directly to your PDF files.

## How PDF QR Codes Work

When someone scans a PDF QR code, here's what happens:

1. **Scan** - The phone's camera reads the QR code pattern
2. **Decode** - The code reveals a URL pointing to your PDF
3. **Redirect** - The browser opens and navigates to that URL
4. **Display** - The PDF renders directly in the mobile browser

Most modern smartphones display PDFs natively - no app installation required. The document loads in seconds, and users can zoom, scroll, and even save it locally if they want.

### Static vs Dynamic PDF QR Codes

**Static QR codes** encode the PDF URL directly into the pattern. The URL is permanent - if you need to change the document, you need a new QR code.

**Dynamic QR codes** point to a redirect URL that you control. This means you can:

- Update the PDF without changing the QR code
- Track scan analytics (location, device, time)
- Set expiration dates
- Add password protection
- A/B test different documents

For most business applications, dynamic QR codes are worth the investment. Learn more about [static vs dynamic QR codes](/learn/static-vs-dynamic-qr-codes).

## Creating a PDF QR Code

### Step 1: Prepare Your PDF

Before generating a QR code, optimize your PDF:

- **File size**: Keep under 10MB for fast loading (under 5MB is ideal)
- **Mobile-friendly**: Use readable fonts (14pt minimum)
- **Orientation**: Portrait works better on phones
- **Resolution**: 150 DPI balances quality and size

**Option B: Use Your Own URL**

### Step 3: Customize Your QR Code

Make your QR code match your brand:

- Choose colors that contrast well (dark on light)
- Add your logo to the center
- Select a pattern style
- Adjust the size for your use case

### Step 4: Test Before Printing

Always test your QR code with multiple devices:

- Different phone models (iPhone and Android)
- Various QR scanner apps
- Different lighting conditions
- The actual print size you'll use

<Callout type="warning" title="Important">
Test at the final print size. A QR code that works on screen might fail when printed small on a business card.
</Callout>

### Step 5: Download and Deploy

Download your QR code in the format you need:

- **PNG**: Best for digital use and most print applications
- **SVG**: Best for large-format printing (scales infinitely)

## Best Practices for PDF QR Codes

### Design for Scannability

- Minimum size: 2cm x 2cm (0.8" x 0.8") for close-range scanning
- Maintain quiet zone (white border) around the code
- Ensure strong contrast between code and background
- Test in the actual environment before mass printing

### Optimize the PDF Experience

- Include a clear title on the first page
- Add navigation for multi-page documents
- Ensure text is selectable (not just images)
- Include contact information on every page

### Provide Context

Don't just place a QR code - tell people what they'll get:

- "Scan for Full Menu"
- "View Product Manual"
- "Download Event Schedule"

### Plan for Updates

If you might update the document, use a dynamic QR code. The small monthly cost pays for itself the first time you avoid reprinting.

### Track Performance

Monitor your QR code analytics to understand:

- How many people scan
- When they scan (time of day, day of week)
- Where they scan (which locations perform best)
- What devices they use

This data helps optimize placement and content.

## Troubleshooting PDF QR Codes

### "The PDF won't load"

- Check file size (too large = slow loading)
- Verify the PDF isn't corrupted
- Test on different networks (might be firewall issue)
- Ensure hosting service is working

### "The QR code won't scan"

- Check print quality (not pixelated or blurry)
- Verify sufficient contrast
- Ensure minimum size requirements
- Clean the printed surface
- Try different lighting

For more solutions, see our guide on [QR code design best practices](/learn/qr-code-design-best-practices).

### "The wrong PDF appears"

- If using dynamic codes, check your QRWolf dashboard
- Verify you didn't accidentally update the wrong code
- Clear browser cache and try again
