## The Evolution of Business Cards

Traditional paper business cards are becoming outdated. They get lost, damaged, or thrown away. QR code business cards solve these problems by creating a seamless digital connection between you and your new contacts.

## Why QR Code Business Cards?

### Never Lose Contact Info Again

When someone receives your physical card, they might:
- Lose it in their pocket
- Forget to enter the details
- Mistype your email
- Throw it away during cleanup

A QR code eliminates all these issues by saving directly to their phone.

### Always Up-to-Date

Changed jobs? New phone number? With a dynamic QR code, update your info once and everyone who scans gets the current version.

### Environmentally Friendly

Reduce paper waste while making a modern impression.

### Measurable Networking

Track how many people actually saved your contact info.

## Types of QR Code Business Cards

### 1. vCard QR Code

The most common type. When scanned, it adds your contact info directly to the phone's address book.

**Includes:**
- Name
- Phone number(s)
- Email address
- Company
- Job title
- Website
- Physical address

### 2. Digital Business Card Page

A more comprehensive approach with a landing page that includes:
- Profile photo
- Bio
- All contact methods
- Social media links
- Portfolio links
- Calendar booking

### 3. LinkedIn QR Code

Simply links to your LinkedIn profile for professional networking.

## Design Best Practices

### QR Code Placement

| Position | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| Back center | Clean front, large code | May be overlooked |
| Front corner | Immediately visible | Competes with other info |
| Full back | Maximum size | No other back content |

### Size Guidelines

- **Minimum**: 0.8" x 0.8" (2cm x 2cm)
- **Recommended**: 1" x 1" (2.5cm x 2.5cm)
- **Optimal**: As large as design allows

### Branding

- Match QR code colors to your brand
- Add your logo to the QR code center
- Maintain sufficient contrast
- Use consistent fonts

### Call-to-Action

Add text near the QR code:
- "Scan to save my contact"
- "Scan to connect"
- "Save my info instantly"

## Physical Card Integration

### Traditional Business Card + QR

Keep your standard business card design but add a QR code. This works well for:
- Conservative industries
- Clients who prefer paper
- Situations where phones aren't handy

### QR-Focused Card

Minimize text and make the QR code the star. Include:
- Your name
- Job title
- Large QR code
- "Scan to connect"

### NFC + QR Combo

Some modern cards include both NFC chips and QR codes for maximum compatibility.

## Common Mistakes

### 1. QR Code Too Small
If people struggle to scan, they'll give up. Make it large enough.

### 2. Low Contrast
Dark QR on dark background won't scan. Ensure high contrast.

### 3. Cluttered Design
Don't crowd the QR code. Give it breathing room.

### 4. Static QR Codes
Using static codes means you can't update info or track scans.

### 5. Missing Call-to-Action
Tell people what the code does. "Scan to save contact" is clear.
