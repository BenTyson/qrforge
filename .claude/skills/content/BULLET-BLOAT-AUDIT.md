## Key Patterns Identified

### What GOOD articles do:
- Open sections with explanatory paragraphs that set context
- Use concrete examples and scenarios
- Reserve bullets for truly sequential steps or quick-reference summaries
- Have narrative flow between sections
- Explain the "why" behind concepts

### What SEVERE articles do wrong:
- Every heading immediately followed by bullets
- No explanatory prose before lists
- Use bullets for concepts that should be paragraphs
- Read like internal notes or outlines
- Feel like formatted lists of facts, not articles

## Example: Good vs. Bad Structure

### BAD (from a SEVERE article):
```markdown
## Benefits of QR Codes for Healthcare

Healthcare facilities use QR codes for:

- **Patient check-in** - Scan to verify appointment
- **Medical records** - Access patient history
- **Prescription information** - View drug details
- **Wayfinding** - Navigate large facilities
- **Emergency information** - Quick access to protocols
```

### GOOD (what it should be):
```markdown
## Benefits of QR Codes for Healthcare

Healthcare facilities face a unique challenge: they must balance patient
privacy with the need for quick information access. QR codes thread this
needle elegantly, providing instant access to information while keeping
sensitive data secure behind authentication.

Consider patient check-in. Traditional processes involve clipboards,
handwritten forms, and manual data entry—each step introducing both
delays and error opportunities. A QR code at the entrance transforms
this experience. Patients scan, verify their identity through their
phone's biometrics, and their appointment confirms automatically.
No shared pens, no illegible handwriting, no front-desk bottleneck.

The same principle extends throughout the care journey...
```
